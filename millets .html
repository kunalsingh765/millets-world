What Are Millets?

Millets are a group of highly variable small-seeded grasses, widely grown around the world as cereal crops or grains for human food and livestock fodder. They are known for their nutritional value and health benefits.

Types of Millets

  • Finger Millet (Ragi): Rich in calcium and great for bone health.
  • Foxtail Millet: High in protein and dietary fiber.
  • Sorghum (Jowar): Known for its high iron content and energy-boosting properties.
  • Bajra (Pearl Millet): Good source of magnesium, which supports heart health.

Health Benefits of Millets

Millets are gluten-free, rich in fiber, and packed with essential nutrients, making them ideal for people looking to improve their digestion, boost heart health, and manage diabetes.
